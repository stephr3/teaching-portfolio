Statement of Goals

2022 marked a return to (almost) normalcy with full-time in-person classes. I taught Academic Listening and Speaking, Communication Core, and IT Literacy. My biggest challenge this year was learning how to incorporate all of the required elements of our Communication Core classes into a 2-day per week format. It was quite challenging to cover so much ground for myself and the students.

For next semester, my goal is to focus on small, attainable goals for the students each week, with clear assessment and feedback. With so much ground to cover, I'd like to make sure that each curriculum goal and its reasoning is clearly addressed for students. I will also be teaching two IT Literacy sections next semester. This could pose some interesting opportunities to share work across sections, creating meaningful portfolio work for students.

I will also continue to collaborate with the Digital Media committee in the fall. Our goal for our videos will be to support the core curriculum as well as provide students technical tips, models for class assignments, and advice for fruitful English Plaza discussion. I'm excited to work on these bite-sized videos which will be reusable throughout future semesters to help guide students.

Finally, in September 2022 my colleague Alexis Busso and I will begin conducting research regarding the effect of metacognitive strategies on vocabulary retention. This has been a large project to prepare materials for, and I'm looking forward to being able to collect real data from the students so that we can start analyzing our results. We plan to present our initial findings at a vocabulary conference held at TIU in October.
